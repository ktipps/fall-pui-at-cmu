Reducing Self-Consciousness in Remote Classroms


User Research Lead & Project Manager

Aug. - Dec. 2021



How might we reduce graduate students' self-consciousness in remote classrooms?



When COVID-19 hit in 2019, students and teachers around the world were forced to abondon their classrooms in favor of remote learning environments like Zoom.

The Problem

Video conferencing platforms assist students’ learning, but they also leave some people feeling self-conscious about their appearance, background, and general performance in a recorded environment. These factors can distract students and negatively impact their learning.

The Solution

A design intervention that leverages digital avatars and self-affirming prompts to reduce physical judgements about apperance, bolster graduate students’ self-esteem and confidence, and preserve important body language cues.
